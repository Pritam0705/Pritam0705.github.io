01

RAG System for Property Tax Documents

LLM / RAG
Situation

Researchers at UChicago Booth needed to query 5,000+ property tax PDFs for policy analysis — a process that took weeks of manual review per research question.

Task

Design a system that lets researchers ask natural language questions and get accurate, sourced answers from the full document corpus.

Approach

Built a RAG pipeline using LangChain for orchestration, Qdrant as vector store for document embeddings, and GPT-4o for generation with prompt engineering for citation accuracy.

Result

Reduced manual research effort by 80%. Researchers could query the full corpus in seconds, accelerating policy analysis cycles from weeks to hours.

02

Spatiotemporal Parking Forecasting (PNNL)

Time Series
Situation

The Department of Energy needed accurate curbside parking availability predictions for smart city mobility planning — existing models couldn't capture spatial dependencies between adjacent streets.

Task

Develop a forecasting framework that models both temporal patterns and spatial relationships in parking occupancy data.

Approach

Designed a hybrid GCNN + LSTM architecture to capture spatial graph structure and temporal sequences. Benchmarked against 6 baselines including Prophet, N-BEATS, and Random Forest.

Result

Achieved 90%+ prediction accuracy, outperforming the best baseline by ~10%. Delivered findings to DOE stakeholders for mobility planning integration.

Energy Demand Forecasting for EV Stations

Time Series
Situation

EV charging stations needed accurate energy demand forecasts to optimize resource allocation and estimate GHG savings.

Task

Compare forecasting approaches and identify the best model for granular energy consumption prediction.

Approach

Evaluated tree-based models (RF: 72.3%), linear models (64%), and deep learning. Tested N-BEATS and LSTM for temporal dependency capture.

Result

LSTM achieved 22% MAPE, N-BEATS 28.9% — both significantly outperformed baselines, validating deep learning for granular energy forecasting.

BERT Text Classification — Native Language ID

NLP
Situation

Needed to predict authors' native languages from their English writing — a challenging NLP task requiring deep contextual understanding of writing patterns.
